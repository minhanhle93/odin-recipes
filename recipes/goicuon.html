Goi Cuon

Description

Goi Cuon, often referred to as Vietnamese fresh spring rolls, is a delightful dish that embodies the essence of Vietnamese cuisine in every bite. These translucent, delicate rice paper rolls are filled with a medley of fresh and vibrant ingredients, making them a perfect embodiment of the country's love for wholesome and balanced flavors. To create Goi Cuon, tender rice paper sheets are soaked briefly in warm water, making them pliable for rolling.

A traditional filling comprises thinly sliced shrimp, fragrant herbs like mint and Thai basil, rice vermicelli noodles, and often, tender strips of poached pork or tofu for a vegetarian option. The art of rolling these treasures lies in folding the sides inward, then carefully enclosing the fillings to form a neat, cylindrical package. Served with a dipping sauce typically made from hoisin sauce, peanut butter, and a hint of chili, Goi Cuon is a refreshingly light and healthful appetizer or snack that captures the essence of Vietnamese cuisine's freshness and balance.

Ingredients

For the Filling:

  • Rice paper wrappers
  • Cooked and peeled shrimps, halved lengthwise
  • Rice vermicelli noodles
  • Fresh herbs like mint, Thai basil, and cilantro
  • Sliced cucumber
  • Julienne-cut carrots
  • Thinly sliced lettuce or cabbage
  • Thinly sliced pork

For the Dipping Sauce:

  • Hoisin sauce
  • Peanut butter
  • Sugar
  • Water
  • Lime juice
  • Minced garlic
  • Crushed roasted peanuts

Steps

  1. Prepare the filling ingredients
  2. Soak rice paper wrappers in warm water until pliable
  3. Assemble the spring rolls by placing fillings on the wrappers and rolling them up
  4. Serve with dipping sauce and enjoy!
