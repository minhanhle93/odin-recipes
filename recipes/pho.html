Pho

Description

Pho, a beloved Vietnamese noodle soup, is a tantalizing fusion of aromatic herbs, rich beef or chicken broth, and slender rice noodles. This iconic dish is a harmonious blend of flavors and textures that dance on your taste buds. To create the perfect pho, start by simmering bones, such as beef knuckles or chicken carcasses, with a medley of charred onions and ginger, infusing the broth with a deep, savory essence.

A symphony of spices like star anise, cinnamon, and cloves join the ensemble, contributing their warm, fragrant notes. Once the broth is ready, it's poured over thinly sliced raw beef or cooked chicken, allowing the heat to gently cook the proteins. Garnished with fresh herbs, bean sprouts, lime wedges, and fiery Sriracha, each spoonful of pho is a captivating journey through the culinary traditions of Vietnam.

Ingredients

For the Broth:

  • Beef bones
  • Onion
  • Ginger
  • Cinnamon sticks
  • Star anise
  • Black cardamom
  • Coriander seeds
  • Fennel seeds

For the Noodles and Toppings:

  • Pho noodles
  • Thinly sliced raw beef
  • Fresh herbs like cilantro, Thai basil, and mint
  • Lime wedges
  • Sliced jalapenos
  • Hoisin sauce
  • Sriracha sauce

Steps

  1. Prepare the broth with charred ingredients and spices
  2. Cook the rice noodles separately
  3. Slice raw beef and prepare fresh herbs and toppings
  4. Assemble by placing noodles and raw beef in a bowl
  5. Pour hot broth over the noodles and beef
  6. Add herbs, bean sprouts, and condiments to taste
  7. Serve and enjoy!
